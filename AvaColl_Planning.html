1About Avacor Ltd▼

Avacor Ltd was incorporated in October 2011 and began operations the same year. The management and operations team are drawn from the pioneers of the local security industry, with over three decades of combined experience in the Mauritian security sector.

Avacor Ltd is headquartered at 5, de Courson Street, Curepipe, Mauritius, and operates a fleet of 35 vehicles across the island with a team of approximately 790 security personnel.

The company provides three core services: Man Guarding, Alarm Monitoring & Mobile Response, and Electronic Security Systems Supply & Installation. Operations are structured across four zones covering the entire island of Mauritius.

2.2 The Mobile Supervisor Team

The Mobile Supervisor Team is a dedicated operational unit, entirely separate from the on-site guarding team. They operate from company vehicles across the Zone and are responsible for alarm intervention, emergency response, site checks, and guard movements.

GradeShoulder Bars
Chief Security Officer (CSO)3 bars
Security Officer (SO)2 bars
Assistant Security Officer (ASO)1 bar
SupervisorNone
⚠ Important
These grades do NOT create a chain of command within the team. Every member takes instructions directly from the Regional Supervisor, Regional Manager, and Guarding Manager only.
💡 Advisory
Promoting team members through grades is a powerful motivational tool. Ensure criteria for promotion are transparent and consistently applied.
2.3 Reporting Lines

All members report directly to: Regional Supervisor, Regional Manager, and Guarding Manager (Mr. Thillay Rody — Zone 1). For day-to-day operational instructions, supervisors also take direction from the Control Room and CMS department.

3Roster, Planning & Leave▼

The shift roster and deployment planning is managed by the Control Department or the Guarding Manager. Supervisors do not self-assign shifts or deployments.

  • All leave requests must be submitted directly to the Guarding Manager.
  • Leave requests must be made in advance — do not assume leave is approved until confirmed in writing.
  • Absence from duty without prior approval is a serious disciplinary matter.
💡 Advisory
The Guarding Manager should maintain a leave register and give supervisors reasonable notice of their roster. Last-minute roster changes are disruptive — plan ahead where possible.

4.2 Attendance Rules
  • Supervisors must report 15 minutes before shift start for vehicle takeover, equipment check, and WhatsApp handover reporting.
  • A supervisor must not leave their post until fully relieved by the incoming shift supervisor.
  • Any anticipated lateness must be reported to the Guarding Manager or Control Room immediately.
  • Absence must be communicated at least 2 hours before shift commencement.
💡 Advisory
Punctuality is a direct reflection of professionalism. A supervisor who is late creates an uncovered operational gap. Habitual lateness will be addressed through the DS process.
5Vehicle Takeover Procedure▼

Each Mobile Supervisor is assigned a branded Avacor Ltd company vehicle for their shift. The supervisor is fully responsible for the vehicle and all equipment from the moment of takeover until handover at shift end.

StepActionDetails
1Photograph the vehicleTake clear photographs from all sides — front, back, left, right, and any existing damage. This protects you from being held responsible for pre-existing damage.
2Send photos to Vehicle Handover WhatsApp groupSend all photographs immediately to the designated Vehicle Handover WhatsApp group — your formal record of the vehicle's condition.
3Inspect vehicle exteriorWalk around the full vehicle. Check for any new damage, scratches, or dents not visible in previous handover photos. Report any discrepancy immediately.
4Check vehicle equipmentTorch, Tonfa baton, Zone key batch, Diary/notebook, Vehicle logbook, Company smartphone (fully charged), Clocking device (fully charged). Also check: all four tyres, spare tyre, wheel kit, all headlights, and gyrophare/roof beacon.
5Check vehicle logbook & diaryConfirm the logbook and diary are up to date from the previous shift and ready for use.
6Confirm takeover on WhatsAppPost on Zone Supervision WhatsApp group: takeover complete, any deficiencies noted, commencing shift.
⚠ Important
Do not commence your shift without completing the full takeover. If equipment is missing or the vehicle is damaged and not previously reported, you become responsible. Always document and report before moving off.
💡 Advisory
Consider keeping a standard takeover checklist card in the vehicle that the incoming supervisor physically ticks off. This reduces errors and ensures nothing is missed.
6Guard Movement Procedure▼

Guard movements are one of the core duties of the Mobile Supervisor Team. Supervisors transport guards who have no means of personal transport from their home or a designated pickup point to their allocated site.

6.1 How Guard Movements Are Assigned
  • After completing vehicle takeover, the supervisor must immediately call the Control Room.
  • The Control Room holds all deployment planning — they know who is on duty, who has been replaced, and who requires transport.
  • The supervisor does not decide guard movements independently. All movements are instructed by the Control Room.
⚠ Important
Never perform a guard movement without instruction from the Control Room and the Guarding Manager's approval. Acting on assumptions leads to operational confusion and gaps in site coverage.
6.2 Step-by-Step Procedure
StepActionDetails
1Call Control RoomAt the start of shift, call the Control Room and request the movement list for your zone.
2Receive instructionsControl Room advises which guards require transport, pickup locations, and destination sites.
3Post on Zone WhatsApp group"Proceeding for [Guard Name] movement from [Location] to [Site Name]."
4Collect the guardProceed to the guard's home or designated pickup point. No unnecessary stops or diversions.
5Drop off at siteTransport the guard directly to their allocated site or designated drop-off point.
6Confirm drop-off on WhatsApp"Dropped [Guard Name] at [Site Name] — [Time]."
7Proceed to next taskContact Control Room to confirm completion and receive the next task.
⚠ Priority Rule
At any point during a guard movement, if an alarm intervention is received, the supervisor must immediately prioritise the alarm. Inform the Control Room, post on the Zone WhatsApp group, and proceed to the alarm location without delay. Guard movements resume after the alarm intervention is completed.
7Alarm Intervention Procedure▼

Alarm intervention is the highest-priority operational duty of the Mobile Supervisor Team. All alarms are monitored by the CMS department (Central Monitoring Station).

7.1 How an Alarm is Received

CMS monitors all alarm systems across Zone 1. When an alarm activates, the CMS operator identifies the nearest available supervisor vehicle and calls the supervisor directly with the alarm name and location. CMS also sends the location via WhatsApp.

7.2 Response Time Standard
StandardRequirement
Maximum response time25 minutes from alarm received to arrival on site
Over 25 minutesClassified as a DELAYED intervention — recorded as a performance issue
7.3 Equipment to Carry During Intervention
  • Tonfa baton — for personal protection
  • Torchlight — mandatory during night shift
  • Zone key batch — to access authorised premises
7.4 Step-by-Step Procedure
StepActionDetails
1Receive alarm from CMSCMS calls you with alarm name and location, and sends location via WhatsApp.
2Post on Zone WhatsApp"Alarm received — [Location] — [Exact time received]."
3Proceed to locationDrive to the alarm location. Must arrive within 25 minutes.
4Call CMS on arrivalCall CMS to confirm your presence on site. State your arrival time.
5External perimeter checkFull walk around the outside. Look for: signs of forced entry, broken windows, open gates or doors, suspicious persons or vehicles, or any danger.
6Assess for dangerIf any sign of danger or intrusion — DO NOT ENTER. Step back to a safe distance. Proceed to step 7. If no danger, proceed to step 8.
7If danger observedCall CMS immediately. CMS will coordinate the appropriate response (police, management). If CMS unreachable, call police directly (999). Await instructions. Do not act alone.
8Access the compoundUsing the zone key batch, access the compound where authorised. Conduct a thorough inspection of the premises.
9Take photographsWhether all is normal or abnormalities are found — take photographs as proof of attendance. Mandatory on every intervention.
10Note all abnormalitiesOpen doors, open windows, unauthorized vehicle, person on premises, power blackout, damaged property, forced entry, or any unusual finding.
11Give final verbal report to CMSCall CMS and provide a full verbal report of your findings. CMS will formally close the alarm.
12Post final report on Zone WhatsAppSend the same report on the Zone WhatsApp group so all managers are informed.
7.5 Non-Negotiable Rules
⚠ Important
A supervisor must NEVER enter a building or house, even if a door or window is found open. External inspection only. Report findings — do not investigate inside.
  • Never enter any building without explicit authorisation, regardless of what is found outside.
  • Never remove any object from the compound under any circumstances.
  • Personal safety comes first at all times. If in doubt, withdraw and call for support.
💡 Advisory
If false alarms are recurring at a specific site, raise this with the Guarding Manager. Repeat false alarms waste response capacity and may indicate a faulty system that should be flagged to the client.
8Site Check Procedure▼

Site checks ensure that guards are performing their duties correctly, that client sites are secured, and that any issues are identified and addressed promptly.

8.1 How Site Checks Are Assigned

Site checks are conducted randomly — there is no fixed pattern or schedule. Supervisors are directed to specific sites by the Regional Supervisor or Guarding Manager during both day and night shifts.

💡 Advisory
Random, unannounced checks are the most effective way to maintain guard standards. Vary your routes, times, and sites deliberately.
8.2 Step-by-Step Procedure
StepActionDetails
1Announce arrival on Zone WhatsApp"On site — [Site Name] — [Time]."
2Entry in site diary bookRecord your name, the date, and your exact arrival time.
3Check guard presenceConfirm guard is physically present at their designated post. An absent guard is an immediate issue — report to Control Room straight away.
4Check guard grooming & uniformCorrect uniform worn, shirt clean and ironed, black belt, white cap on straight, black shoes polished, ID badge visible, shoulder badge correct for rank.
5Check guard conduct & alertnessAre they focused? Using a personal phone? Eating or engaging in inappropriate behaviour? Address any issue immediately.
6Check equipment on postTorch, radio, clocking device, access control equipment, keys — present and functional.
7Verify clocking patrolsCheck that patrol clocking records are being completed as per the scheduled patrol plan. Gaps must be documented and reported.
8Check all site documentationSite diary book, log book, vehicle book (if applicable). Incorrect or empty records must be corrected immediately.
9Check access points & gate securityAll access points secured per site instructions, gates closed and locked (especially night shift), perimeter intact.
10Conduct patrol with guardWalk the full premises alongside the guard. Check every area: perimeter, parking, entry points, blind spots.
11Brief guard if new to siteIf newly assigned, conduct a full briefing: site-specific SOP, walk through every post and access point, Emergency Action Plan.
12Accompany new recruitsAny newly recruited guard must be accompanied for a full familiarisation walkthrough, including the Emergency Action Plan. Do not leave a new recruit alone on a site they have not been properly briefed on.
13Record findings in site diary bookBefore leaving: what you checked, what you found, any issues identified, and what action was taken or is required.
14Issue a DS if misconduct observedIf the guard is found in breach of their duties (see Section 9), the DS process must be initiated before you leave the site.
15Post report on Zone WhatsAppSite name, time of visit, findings, any abnormalities, actions taken, and any follow-up required.

9Daily State (DS) Procedure▼

When a supervisor observes a guard neglecting their duties or engaging in misconduct, a Daily State (DS) must be issued. The DS creates an official record of the offence and enables the Guarding Manager to take appropriate disciplinary action.

9.1 Offences That Require a DS
  • Sleeping on post
  • Leaving post without authorisation
  • Wearing incorrect or incomplete uniform
  • Not shaved / poor personal grooming
  • Not using company equipment
  • Using a personal mobile phone while on post
  • Insubordination or disrespectful conduct
  • Any other misconduct observed on site
9.2 Step-by-Step DS Procedure
StepActionDetails
1Observe and confirm the offenceClearly establish what the guard has done or failed to do. Be factual — personal opinions have no place in a DS.
2Call the Control RoomReport the nature of the offence, the guard's name, their site, and the date and time of the offence.
3DS number issuedThe Control Room operator records the offence in the DS Book and issues a DS reference number to the supervisor.
4Record the DS numberNote the DS number in the site diary book for your own records.
5Inform the guardInform the guard calmly and professionally that a DS is being raised against them. State the reason clearly. Do not argue or lose composure.
6Guarding Manager notifiedThe Guarding Manager is notified via the Control Room record and will determine what disciplinary action is appropriate.
⚠ Important
Supervisors do not have the authority to dismiss or suspend a guard. The supervisor's role is to observe, report, and document. The Guarding Manager takes all disciplinary decisions.
💡 Advisory
Do not ignore misconduct to avoid conflict. Failing to raise a DS when warranted undermines standards across the team. A culture of accountability starts with consistent enforcement at supervisor level.
10Sick Guard Movement Procedure▼

When a guard on post reports sick and is unable to continue their duty, the supervisor is responsible for transporting them to the nearest hospital and managing the process through to resolution.

⚠ Important — Hotel Sites
At hotel sites, a sick guard must be REPLACED FIRST before being removed from the post. The site must not be left unmanned. The only exception is a genuine life-threatening emergency.
StepActionDetails
1Guard reports sickThe guard calls the Control Room directly from their post to request medical assistance.
2Control Room notifies supervisorThe Control Room contacts the zone supervisor and instructs a hospital movement.
3Management informedThe Control Room or supervisor ensures the Guarding Manager and Regional Manager are made aware.
4Replacement arranged if neededFor hotel sites, a guard swap is coordinated before the sick guard is removed.
5Post on Zone WhatsApp"Proceeding for sick guard movement — [Guard Name] — [Site] — [Time]."
6Arrive at sitePost on WhatsApp: "Arrived at [Site Name] — [Time]."
7Remove guard from siteOnce replacement is in place, escort the sick guard to the vehicle. Post: "Removing [Guard Name] from [Site Name] — [Time]."
8Proceed to hospitalTransport the guard to the nearest hospital. Drive safely.
9Arrive at hospitalPost on WhatsApp: "Arrived at hospital with [Guard Name] — [Time]."
10Update WhatsApp throughoutPost any updates as they occur — waiting, being seen, outcome.
11Guard unfit certificateGuard is permitted to go home. Transport home is at the guard's own expense and arrangement. Post outcome on WhatsApp.
12Guard declared fit for dutySupervisor collects guard from hospital and returns them to their allocated site to resume duties. Post outcome on WhatsApp.
11Incident Response Procedures▼

Core principle for all incident types: secure the site, report immediately, gather information, and await instruction from the Guarding Manager. The supervisor is never the final decision-maker in a serious incident.

11.1 General Incident Response — Universal Procedure
StepActionDetails
1Incident reportedGuard or officer on site calls Control Room (overnight) or Guarding Manager directly. Control Room escalates to the zone supervisor.
2Post on Zone WhatsApp"Incident reported — [Nature] — [Site Name] — [Time]."
3Notify management if urgentDepending on seriousness, inform the Regional Supervisor, Regional Manager, or Guarding Manager directly.
4Proceed to site immediatelyDrive to the site without delay.
5Arrive on sitePost on WhatsApp: "On site — [Site Name] — [Time]."
6Entry in site diary bookRecord your arrival time and the nature of the incident.
7Gather maximum informationSpeak to the guard, the officer in charge, and the client representative. Gather as much factual information as possible.
8Secure the siteEnsure all guards remain on their posts. Security operations must continue at all times.
9Take photographs where applicablePhotograph the scene, any evidence, damage, or relevant areas.
10Do not decide aloneNo significant action without prior guidance from the Guarding Manager.
11Fire or medical emergencyCall emergency services immediately (999). Do not wait for authorisation — lives take priority.
12Detailed report in diary bookBefore leaving: timeline, people involved, findings, actions taken, emergency service reference numbers.
13Post final report on Zone WhatsAppFull report so all managers are informed of the outcome and any follow-up required.
11.2 Larceny / Theft
⚠ Crime Scene Preservation
In any case involving damaged property, forced entry, or evidence — secure the area immediately. Nothing must be touched, moved, or tampered with pending police arrival. Non-negotiable.
  • Before calling the police, the client must be informed and asked whether they wish to file a case. The decision rests with the client.
  • Proceed to the exact location where the theft occurred. Photograph everything.
  • Check for CCTV cameras covering that location — note availability and angles.
  • Conduct a full patrol of the compound perimeter with the guard, equipped with tonfa baton and torchlight.
11.3 Fire
  • Call 999 immediately — do not wait for instructions.
  • Inform the Guarding Manager.
  • Ensure evacuation is underway as per the site Emergency Action Plan.
  • Prevent re-entry to the building. Do not allow re-entry until the fire service formally declares the building safe.
  • Meet the fire service on arrival and provide all available information.
11.4 Medical Emergency
  • Call 999 immediately for an ambulance — no delay.
  • Inform the Guarding Manager.
  • If trained, administer basic first aid. Use the site AED if available and needed.
  • Do not leave the person unattended.
  • Send a guard to the main entrance to guide the ambulance crew directly to the patient.
  • Provide a clear handover to paramedics: what happened, time, any first aid given.
11.5 Intrusion / Unauthorised Person on Site
  • Do not confront an intruder alone.
  • Observe from a safe distance and report to Control Room and Guarding Manager immediately.
  • Call 999 if the intruder poses any threat to persons or property.
  • If safe to do so and with backup, politely challenge and request the person to leave.
  • Do not physically restrain or block the person.
  • Secure the perimeter and ensure other posts are maintained.
💡 Advisory
Overnight incidents are the most challenging because management support is not immediately on-site. This is precisely why supervisors must know these procedures without having to think. Regular briefings and scenario drills will build this confidence in the team.

14End of Shift & Handover Procedure▼

At the end of every shift, the outgoing supervisor must complete a proper handover to the incoming supervisor. The shift is not complete until handover is done.

StepActionDetails
1Remain on duty until relievedDo not leave until the incoming supervisor has physically taken over. No early departures.
2Hand over the vehicleHand over with all equipment: torch, tonfa baton, zone key batch, diary, vehicle logbook, company smartphone, and any other items. All equipment must be fully charged — handing over uncharged equipment is a dereliction of duty.
3Vehicle photographsThe incoming supervisor is responsible for photographing the vehicle and posting the handover record. You do not need to take photos at handover — only at takeover.
4Post end-of-shift summary on Zone WhatsAppAll activities performed, any incidents or abnormalities, any outstanding matters requiring follow-up, and any information the incoming supervisor needs to know.
5Handover completeOnce the incoming supervisor confirms takeover, your shift is complete.
💡 Advisory
A thorough handover message on WhatsApp is not just a formality — it ensures continuity of operations and protects you if any issue from your shift is raised later. Be detailed and factual.
15General Conduct & Professional Standards▼
15.1 Professional Conduct
  • Supervisors must be polite, respectful, and professional at all times — with guards, clients, and members of the public.
  • Personal mobile phones must not be used for non-work purposes during a shift.
  • Eating, drinking (except water), or smoking is not permitted while on operational duty.
  • Supervisors must not sleep or rest during active shift hours.
  • Consumption of alcohol or any intoxicating substance before or during duty is strictly prohibited and is grounds for immediate suspension.
15.2 Authority & Decision-Making
  • Supervisors must not take important decisions independently during incidents. The Guarding Manager is the authority.
  • Supervisors have no authority to dismiss, suspend, or impose punishments on guards beyond initiating the DS process.
  • Supervisors must never act in a manner that could expose the company to legal or reputational risk.
15.3 Use of the Company Vehicle
  • The vehicle is a company asset and must be treated with care at all times. No personal use permitted.
  • No unauthorised passengers may be transported in the vehicle.
  • Any accident, scratch, or damage must be reported immediately to the Guarding Manager — no exceptions and no delays.
  • Any road traffic fines are the personal financial responsibility of the driver. Avacor Ltd will not cover any traffic fines.
  • Maximum driving speed: 70 km/h at all times. Exceeding this limit will result in disciplinary action and salary deduction.
💡 Advisory
The company vehicle carries the Avacor Ltd brand. How it is driven and presented in public reflects directly on the company's image. Drive professionally at all times.
